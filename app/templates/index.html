A Lightweight Prioritization Framework for Startups

VECTR is specifically designed to help software startups make critical product roadmap decisions under severe resource constraints and extreme uncertainty.

The framework was validated by 17 practitioners (Founders, Product Managers, Technical Leads) and proven to be intuitive, visually clear, and effective.

The Three Core Criteria

1. Return on Investment (ROI)

Return on Investment (ROI) quantifies the expected financial gain, or Expected Profit, that a feature will generate relative to the Total Investment Cost. This estimation is typically made over a defined time horizon H.

The gains (Profit) considered in this calculation can include increased revenue, churn reduction, or cost savings.

Conversely, the investment costs (Total Investment Cost) cover both development effort (hours) and associated hourly costs, as well as operational expenses (OPEX) and other direct costs.

2. Time-to-Value (TtV)

Time-to-Value (TtV) represents how quickly a feature can be developed, launched, and subsequently begin delivering measurable business value. This is a crucial criterion for software startups to effectively manage their operational 'runway'.

The TtV is the sum of two components: Time-to-Market and Time-to-Business Value: Time-to-Market is the period required to develop and launch the feature. Time-to-Business Value is the time that passes from the launch until measurable business outcomes werden achieved, such as generating the first revenue, improving user retention, or achieving a specific Key Performance Indicator (KPI).

3. Confidence Level

The Confidence level in the VECTR framework represents the certainty in the underlying assumptions for a given requirement. This confidence is assessed based on the quality of evidence.

The scoring uses a specific range to quantify the strength of the evidence, from 0.0 (No Longer Relevant), where evidence is outdated or invalid, up to 10.0 (Launch Data), which represents the strongest evidence derived from real usage data after launch. Emphasizing confidence supports data-driven decision-making and helps prevent the misallocation of scarce resources by exposing ideas that appear highly promising but reveal critical weaknesses after validation.

The Prioritization Map

VECTR is operationalized as a two-dimensional map that makes trade-offs transparent:

  • X-axis: Represents the Confidence Level.
  • Y-axis: Represents Time-to-Value (TtV).
  • Bubble Size: Represents the Return on Investment (ROI).

Items in the upper-right corner (high Confidence, short TtV) and with larger bubbles (high ROI) are the "Quick Wins". The map's color zones-ranging from Green to Red provide instant guidance. Importantly, the position of a feature is dynamic: Validation moves features to the right, increasing confidence and helping to prevent misallocation of scarce resources to high-risk ideas.
