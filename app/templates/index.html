A Lightweight Prioritization Framework for Startups

VECTR is specifically designed to help software startups make critical product roadmap decisions under severe resource constraints and extreme uncertainty.

The framework was validated by 17 practitioners (Founders, Product Managers, Technical Leads) and proven to be intuitive, visually clear, and effective.

The Three Core VECTR Criteria

1. Return on Investment (ROI)

ROI quantifies the expected financial gain a feature will generate relative to its total investment cost (revenue increase, churn reduction, or cost savings).

2. Time-to-Value (TtV)

TtV represents how quickly a feature can be developed, launched, and begin delivering measurable business value. This is crucial for managing a startup's operational 'runway'.

3. Confidence Level

Measures the reliability of the feature's underlying assumptions based on the quality of supporting evidence and validation progress.

The VECTR Prioritization Map

VECTR is operationalized as a two-dimensional map that makes trade-offs transparent:

  • X-axis: Represents the Confidence Level.
  • Y-axis: Represents Time-to-Value (TtV).
  • Bubble Size: Represents the Return on Investment (ROI).

Items in the upper-right corner (high Confidence, short TtV) and with larger bubbles (high ROI) are the "Quick Wins".
