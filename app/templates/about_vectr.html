VECTR: Prioritize What Really Matters

A Lightweight Prioritization Framework

VECTR is a prioritization framework specifically designed to help software startups make critical product roadmap decisions, even under severe resource constraints. It integrates three core criteria that are highly relevant to early-stage company success.

VECTR has been validated by 17 practitioners (including Founders, Product Managers, and Technical Leads) and is valued for being intuitive, visually clear, and useful for structuring product discussions.

The Three Core VECTR Criteria

1. Return on Investment (ROI)

ROI measures the expected financial gain a feature will generate relative to its cost (time, development effort, resources).

  • Goal: Quantify the potential business value.
  • Focus: Maximize financial returns and profitability.

2. Time-to-Value (TtV)

TtV represents how quickly a feature can be developed, launched, and begin delivering real value to the user or business.

  • Goal: Prioritize features with a low TtV.
  • Focus: Enable fast market validation and rapid feedback loops, essential for startups.

3. Confidence Level

The Confidence Level measures the reliability of the assumptions underlying the feature based on the quality and quantity of supporting evidence.

  • Goal: Drive data-driven decision-making.
  • Hierarchy: Evidence like 'Launch Data' (10.0) is considered much stronger than 'Self Conviction' (0.01).
