VECTR: Prioritize What Really Matters

A Lightweight Prioritization Framework

VECTR is a prioritization framework specifically designed to help software startups make critical product roadmap decisions under severe resource constraints. It integrates three core criteria highly relevant to startup success: Return on Investment (ROI), Time-to-Value (TtV), and Confidence.

VECTR was validated by 17 practitioners, including Founders, Product Managers, and Technical Leads. Practitioners found it intuitive, visually clear, and useful for structuring product discussions. The framework directly addresses the startup challenge of prioritizing under extreme uncertainty and limited resources.

The Three Core VECTR Criteria

1. Return on Investment (ROI)

ROI measures the expected financial gain a feature will generate relative to its total investment cost. It may include increased revenue, churn reduction, or cost savings.

  • Goal: Quantify the potential business value.
  • Focus: Maximize financial returns and profitability.

2. Time-to-Value (TtV)

TtV represents how quickly a feature can be developed, launched, and begin delivering measurable business value. This is critical for managing the startup's operational runway.

  • Goal: Prioritize features with a low TtV.
  • Focus: Enable fast market validation and rapid feedback loops, essential for startups.

3. Confidence Level

The Confidence Level measures the reliability of the feature's underlying assumptions based on the quality of supporting evidence and validation progress.

  • Goal: Drive data-driven decision-making.
  • Hierarchy: Evidence like 'Launch Data' (10.0) is considered much stronger dan 'Self Conviction' (0.01).

The VECTR Prioritization Map

VECTR is operationalized as a two-dimensional prioritization map that makes trade-offs transparent.

  • X-axis: Represents the Confidence Level.
  • Y-axis: Represents Time-to-Value (TtV) (where "Fast" is high and "Slow" is low).
  • Bubble Size (Diameter): Represents the Return on Investment (ROI).

Items located in the upper-right (high Confidence, short TtV) and depicted with larger bubbles (high ROI) are the most attractive candidates ("Quick Wins").

Value in Practice

VECTR offers founders and product managers a defensible basis to justify prioritization choices in strategic contexts, such as board meetings and quarterly planning.

  • Reduces Subjectivity: The visual and financial framework helps depersonalize debates and moves decisions from emotion-driven to data-driven.
  • Runway Logic: The explicit link to TtV and ROI introduces a "runway logic" that is crucial for startup survival, unlike frameworks like RICE or ICE.
  • Prevents Misallocation: By emphasizing confidence, VECTR helps prevent the misallocation of scarce resources on assumptions with weak evidence.
